Train the matcher on your own data

Upload an Excel (.xlsx) or CSV file shaped like the common Kaggle-style "symptoms → disease" datasets: one row per patient case, one column per symptom (values 1/0), and one column with the disease name (header called Disease, prognosis, or diagnosis).

This is not deep learning — it's a transparent statistical step: for each disease, it counts how often each symptom shows up in that disease's rows, and turns that frequency into a match weight. That weight then plugs into the same scoring engine you already saw. Honest framing: it's "learning from your data," not a black-box neural network.

What this tool actually does

This checker compares the symptoms you select against a small reference table of common conditions and scores how closely they overlap. It is rule-based pattern matching, not a trained diagnostic model, and it does not examine you, run tests, or know your full medical history.

It will get things wrong. Two very different illnesses can share the same three or four symptoms. A tool like this should narrow your thinking and help you describe what you're feeling — it should never replace a clinician, and it should never be the reason you delay care for something serious.

When to skip this tool entirely and get help now

  • Chest pain or pressure, especially with shortness of breath
  • Difficulty breathing or bluish lips/face
  • Sudden confusion, slurred speech, face drooping, or one-sided weakness
  • Vomiting or coughing up blood
  • Severe, sudden headache unlike any before
  • Signs of a severe allergic reaction (swelling of face/throat, hives with breathing trouble)

In any of these cases, contact your local emergency number immediately.
